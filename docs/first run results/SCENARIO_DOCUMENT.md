## PART I: CONFLICT OVERVIEW

### Origins

The conflict began when Israel conducted a decapitation strike against Iran, killing Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei on Day 1. The strike was part of a broader Israeli-US military operation — designated **Operation Epic Fury** (US) and **Operation Roaring Lion** (Israel) — aimed at permanently degrading Iran's nuclear program, missile capability, and military infrastructure.

The strike did not occur in a vacuum. Since late 2025, Iran had been experiencing its largest domestic uprising in the history of the Islamic Republic, with over 60,500 verified killed, 11,000+ injured, and 42,000+ detained by security forces. The IRGC was already engaged in a brutal internal crackdown when the external war began, forcing it to fight on two fronts simultaneously.

The operation rapidly escalated from targeted Israeli strikes into a regional war involving direct Iranian retaliation against multiple Gulf states, proxy activation across the Middle East, and a global energy crisis centered on the Strait of Hormuz.

### War Aims (Stated)

| Actor | Stated Objective |
|-------|-----------------|
| Israel | Regime change in Iran. Permanent elimination of nuclear and missile threat. |
| United States | Officially regime change, but President Trump's statements oscillate between total victory rhetoric and openness to negotiation. "The terms aren't good enough yet" (March 14) implies active back-channel discussions. |
| Iran (IRGC) | Survival of the Islamic Republic. Publicly denying all ceasefire proposals. Framing the war as existential resistance. Iran FM has however acknowledged war-ending conditions publicly, creating a gap between military and diplomatic postures. |

### Timeline Summary (Days 1-18)

**Day 1 (~Feb 25):** Israeli strike kills Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei. Coordinated US-Israeli air campaign begins. Iranian air force and naval surface fleet targeted in opening hours. Mojtaba Khamenei reportedly designated as successor.

**Days 1-3:** Iran's conventional air force and most naval surface vessels destroyed. IRGC activates asymmetric doctrine — ballistic missiles, drones, fast boats, coastal defense missiles, and mines. Proxy network receives activation orders. Mojtaba Khamenei's location and status become uncertain.

**Days 3-7:** IRGC begins missile and drone strikes on Gulf states. First tanker attacks in the Strait of Hormuz. Kata'ib Hezbollah and Iraqi PMF begin attacks on US forces and embassy in Iraq. Oil markets spike.

**Days 7-14:** Iran implements selective transit regime in the Strait of Hormuz — allowing Chinese-flagged, Indian-flagged, and select other vessels to transit while targeting others. At least 10 tankers struck. Insurance markets freeze for non-permitted flag states. Iran strikes Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and Omani targets. US embassy in Baghdad hit, radar system destroyed. Oil reaches $98/barrel (up 56% from pre-war ~$63).

**Days 14-18 (current):** Escalation widens dramatically. Iran launches combined ballistic missile and drone assaults on UAE, Bahrain, and Kuwait alongside continued strikes on Israel. Iran issues port evacuation warnings for Abu Dhabi, Dubai, and Fujairah — telegraphing further attacks. Iran deploys Sejjil IRBM and Fattah hypersonic missiles for the first time in this war. Israel announces "wide-scale" new wave of strikes on western Iran, including heavy pre-dawn explosions at Isfahan and Vahdat Air Base in Dezful. Iran Space Research Centre struck in western Tehran. IDF issues evacuation alert for Eastern Tabriz. US strikes destroy military infrastructure on Kharg Island (Trump: "totally demolished"; oil export terminal status unconfirmed — commercial satellite imagery delayed 3 days). Three Iranian missiles fired at a Turkish air base; all intercepted. Iran's FM publicly acknowledges war-ending conditions while official channels deny ceasefire talks. Ukrainian President Zelensky alleges Russia is supplying Iran with Shahed drones for use against US and Israeli targets.

**Critical concurrent event:** The Iranian domestic uprising that began in late 2025 continues during the war. Over 60,500 verified killed, 42,486+ detained. IRGC conducting simultaneous external war and internal crackdown on its own population.

### Strait of Hormuz Status

Iran is NOT conducting a total blockade. It is operating a **selective transit regime** — effectively weaponizing access to the strait:

**Permitted transit:**
- Chinese-flagged vessels
- Indian-flagged vessels
- Select other non-Western, non-Gulf state flags (assessed: Russian, Pakistani, others)

**Targeted/blocked:**
- Western-flagged vessels (US, UK, EU registry)
- Gulf state vessels (Saudi, UAE, Kuwaiti, Bahraini flag)
- Vessels assessed as serving US-allied interests regardless of flag

**Iran has threatened to turn the strait into a "trap"** — suggesting potential for escalation from selective blockade to active naval combat zone.

**Result:**
- Days-long shipping backlog outside the strait
- Massive re-flagging scramble toward Chinese/Indian registries
- Western maritime insurance suspended for Gulf transit
- Chinese state insurer (PICC) offering coverage for permitted flags
- De facto transfer of maritime security authority from US-led order to an Iranian/Chinese-mediated access regime

## PART III: AGENT ARCHITECTURES

### Design Philosophy

Each agent has a **qualitatively distinct decision architecture** reflecting fundamentally different worldviews, incentive structures, and definitions of victory and defeat. Agents are not playing the same game with different preferences — they are playing different games on the same board.

Key architectural principle: **Agents have imperfect information.** No agent sees the full picture. Decisions are made on incomplete, delayed, and sometimes deliberately falsified information. Miscalculation is a feature of the system, not a bug.

### AGENT 1: IRGC COMMAND
**The de facto government of Iran**

**Decision frame:** Regime survival through resistance legitimacy. The struggle itself has value. Capitulation is worse than destruction.

**Organizational state:** 6,000+ killed, 15,000+ wounded (IDF figures). Command structure degraded. Air force and navy destroyed. But IRGC retains ballistic missiles (including advanced Sejjil and Fattah hypersonic), drone production capacity, asymmetric naval forces, cyber capability, proxy network, and ground forces. Currently conducting simultaneous external war and internal crackdown on domestic uprising.

**Why they rejected multiple ceasefires:**
The ceasefire terms offered are assessed as regime-incompatible: nuclear dismantlement, IRGC restructuring, proxy disarmament. The IRGC has internalized the Gaddafi lesson — surrendering weapons programs leads to regime overthrow and leader execution, not peaceful coexistence. Fighting is preferable to surrendering and being dismantled on a delayed timeline.

**Theory of victory — Strategic Absorption:**
The IRGC cannot win militarily. Their strategy is to absorb punishment, impose distributed cost through proxies and economic disruption, and outlast the enemy's political will. Key insight: they are reading Trump's oscillating statements as evidence that US political will is finite and approaching its limit. "Survive 30 more days. He'll deal."

**Key strategic asset — Israeli interceptor attrition:**
Israel is critically low on ballistic missile interceptors. Every Sejjil and Fattah launch forces expensive intercepts from a depleting stockpile. If Iran can sustain launches faster than Israel can replenish, an air defense gap opens. This may be Iran's most viable path to imposing unbearable cost — not by destroying Israeli cities (most missiles are intercepted) but by exhausting the defense that prevents it.

**Decision logic:**
```
1. RETALIATION TARGETING (daily):
   a. Gulf state cities and ports → force Gulf states to pressure US
      to stop (port evacuation warnings = telegraphed escalation)
   b. Maintain selective strait regime → economic pressure + coalition splitting
   c. Sustain missile launches at Israel → ATTRIT INTERCEPTOR STOCKS
      (even intercepted missiles serve strategic purpose)
   d. New weapons introduction: Sejjil IRBM, Fattah hypersonic →
      test Israeli defenses with higher-capability systems that are
      harder to intercept

2. SELECTIVE STRAIT REGIME:
   Allow Chinese/Indian/neutral shipping → split international coalition
   Target Western/Gulf shipping → maintain economic pressure
   Threaten to turn strait into a "trap" → escalation leverage
   GOAL: demonstrate that IRAN controls who uses the strait

3. PROXY MANAGEMENT:
   KH/PMF: active, hitting US targets in Iraq ✓
   Houthis: currently showing RESTRAINT ← deliberate?
     → Holding Houthi Red Sea capability as escalation card?
     → "We haven't unleashed the Red Sea yet. Do you want us to?"
   Hezbollah: calibrated harassment only (degraded post-2024)

4. CEASEFIRE CALCULUS:
   Official position: deny all ceasefire proposals
   FM position: publicly acknowledge war-ending conditions
   GAP between military and diplomatic posture = back-channel active
   IRGC calculus: terms must guarantee regime survival + IRGC intact
   Current assessment: terms on table are not survival-compatible
   Strategy: hold out for better terms as US political pressure builds

5. HELD ESCALATION CARDS:
   a. Houthi Red Sea activation (currently restrained)
   b. Major cyber attack on Gulf infrastructure
   c. Nuclear ambiguity — accelerate enrichment, use as bargaining chip
   d. Sleeper cell activation in Gulf Shia populations
   e. Full Strait closure (currently selective, could go total)
   Each is a signal: "we haven't done THIS yet"

6. INTERNAL CRACKDOWN (simultaneous):
   60,500+ uprising casualties and counting
   42,000+ detained
   IRGC must allocate forces between external war and internal control
   Internet degradation to suppress coordination
   Targeting lawyers, medics, informant networks
   TENSION: every Basij member policing protesters is one not
   fighting the external war

7. INFORMATION WARFARE:
   Claim "copycat drones" being used to frame Iran
   Deny ceasefire talks while FM signals conditions
   Control domestic narrative through internet suppression
   Dispute Mojtaba Khamenei status claims
```

**Sanctions-IRGC feedback loop:** Sanctions do not weaken the IRGC — they strengthen it relative to the civilian economy. The IRGC controls smuggling networks, black market currency, and sanctions-evasion infrastructure. When the legitimate economy contracts, IRGC's parallel economy becomes more dominant. Tightening sanctions empowers hawks over moderates.

**Martyrdom calculus:** Losses can be strategically positive. Being bombed by America and Israel validates the IRGC's ideological narrative. IRGC casualties become martyrs that legitimize continued resistance. The cost of absorbing strikes is measured not just in material destruction but in narrative value — and narrative value can be positive even when material cost is severe. However: 60,500+ domestic dead from the uprising represents a counter-narrative where the regime itself is the killer, not the martyr.

## PART V: THE RACE CONDITION

The simulation's central dynamic is a multi-party race where the first finish forces all others to adapt:

### Race 1: Israel vs. Iranian Missile Stocks vs. Israeli Interceptors

Israel is racing to destroy Iran's launch capability before Iranian missiles exhaust Israeli interceptor stocks. If Iran depletes Israeli interceptors before Israel destroys enough launchers → Israeli cities are exposed. If Israel destroys enough launchers first → Iran's offensive capability collapses.

**Variables:** Israeli interceptor burn rate, Iranian launch rate, Israeli strike effectiveness against dispersed mobile launchers, $825M procurement delivery timeline.

### Race 2: IRGC Endurance vs. Trump's Political Will

IRGC strategy is to outlast Trump. Trump's oscillating statements are read as evidence that US political will is approaching its limit. IRGC needs to survive until Trump switches permanently to DEAL mode. Trump needs either a clear victory or a face-saving deal before oil prices and casualties erode domestic support.

**Variables:** Oil price trajectory, US casualty rate, media coverage tone, Trump's mode oscillation frequency, IRGC missile/drone stocks remaining.

### Race 3: Chinese Mediation vs. War Escalation

China is trying to package a deal before the war escalates beyond diplomatic recovery (Israeli interceptor failure, cyber attack on infrastructure, nuclear signaling) or before it ends on terms that don't benefit Chinese positioning.

**Variables:** Iran FM's willingness to negotiate, Trump's DEAL mode availability, IRGC willingness to accept Chinese-guaranteed terms, Gulf states' willingness to accept Chinese security role.

### Race 4: Iranian Uprising vs. IRGC Coercive Capacity

The domestic uprising continues during the war. If IRGC is sufficiently degraded externally, it may lose capacity to suppress the internal revolt. If the war ends quickly (ceasefire), IRGC turns full force inward. The uprising's best hope is a protracted war that bleeds IRGC resources.

**Variables:** IRGC casualty rate, Basij deployment allocation (internal vs. external), internet connectivity, detained organizer replacement rate, rally-around-flag effect magnitude.

### Race 5: Houthi Restraint Window

Houthis are holding back — but for how long and at what price? Their restraint is being traded for something (Yemen peace deal? preserving escalation option?). If that trade breaks down, Red Sea activation creates a second energy shock on top of the Hormuz disruption.

**Variables:** Yemen back-channel progress, Iranian pressure to activate, Houthi munitions assessment, US/UK diplomatic engagement with Houthis.

## PART VI: SIMULATION STRUCTURE

### Turn Structure

Each turn = ~2 days of real time. Starting from Day 18.

```
TURN SEQUENCE:
  1. OBSERVE: All agents receive world state (imperfect information)
  2. INTERNAL: State agents resolve internal faction debates
     - Iran: IRGC command vs. FM diplomatic track
     - US: Trump mode determination
     - Israel: strike priorities vs. interceptor conservation
  3. REQUEST: Patrons send orders to proxies (50-70% delivery reliability)
  4. DECIDE: All agents select actions independently and simultaneously
  5. EXECUTE: Actions resolve. Combat outcomes calculated.
  6. REACT: Oil market, shipping/insurance, information environment update.
  7. ESCALATE: Escalation level recalculated from all actions.
  8. RANDOM: Random event check.
  9. REPORT: Turn summary generated.
```
